## Learned
- The pull conflict was limited to `docs/progress/session-notes/2026-05-11.md`.
- Both versions contained useful project history, so the cleanest resolution was to merge them into one concise note.
- The strongest homepage story is implementation thinking across business systems, data workflows, AI-assisted execution, and human judgment.
- The systems narrative reads cleaner when the visual weight comes from spacing and hierarchy rather than dark contrast.
- A mostly white background makes the site feel more premium and less heavy while keeping the systems-lab atmosphere.
- The secondary pages needed the same spacing, typography, card language, and pale blue accents as the homepage to feel like one site.
- The cross-device workflow should treat GitHub as the recovery anchor and local machines as replaceable working copies.
- The reference direction works best when implemented as maintainable CSS surfaces rather than copied external or watermarked image assets.
- The homepage should look closer to the mockup when it uses restrained rectangular bands and architectural atmosphere, not large decorative control panels.
- Hard borders made the site feel blocky; soft backgrounds, ambient shadows, and gradient transitions better match the desired seamless architectural feeling.
- Using the provided images directly gives the site a stronger architectural atmosphere than trying to recreate the references with CSS-only shapes.
- The desired final look depends on blending text and architecture into one continuous space instead of placing an image module next to text.
- The hero feels more natural when the image fade is broad and gradual; short gradients create visible vertical seams.
